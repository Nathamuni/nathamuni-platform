🧭 What we're building — the requirement in plain words

  • The site is the organized, searchable home of everything you make. Every reel, post and story you publish flows in automatically — your Instagram captions already feed the titles, descriptions and tags, so new content appears on the site without you doing anything.
  • See the real thumbnail before you click. On desktop, hovering any card shows the full, uncropped thumbnail slightly bigger. On mobile, press-and-hold does the same (a normal tap still opens the video). This works on video cards, the homepage moments strip, and the moments wall.
  • Nine AI images give the site your face and its look. Each has a fixed filename and a fixed home: five category tiles, the homepage hero background, the About portrait, the link-share banner, and the books teaser. Three are DONE and already on the site: cat-mind, cat-fitness, cat-ai. Six remain — prompts below, ready to paste.
  • About Me is currently inaccurate — and it will only be fixed with real facts. The current bio was drafted from captions and inference. It gets rewritten ONLY from genuine data you supply via the master prompt below. Until that dossier arrives, nothing gets invented.
  • Captions vs transcripts — the answer. Captions are enough for baseline browsing and search today. If you paste real descriptions or transcripts into the review Excel (any rows, any time), search and the detail pages get much richer. Automatic transcription of your videos is a possible future feature — not needed to keep things working.

🔑 Master data-retrieval prompt

Your source-of-truth dossier

Paste this into ChatGPT AND Gemini (each one knows different things about you). Save each output as a file and give both back to Claude — everything on the site (About, bios, project pages, book page) gets rebuilt from it with zero invented facts.

You are compiling my personal source-of-truth dossier. You have access to our full chat history and your memory about me (Nathamuni).

STRICT RULES:
- Use ONLY facts you actually know about me from our conversations and your memory. Do not invent, assume, embellish, or fill gaps with plausible details.
- If a section has no known facts, write exactly: UNKNOWN — ask me.
- Mark anything you are less than fully sure about with (?) at the end of the line.
- After each fact, note where it came from in brackets, e.g. [you told me while planning my website], [memory].
- Quote me verbatim where possible — my own wording matters more than your summary.

OUTPUT exactly this markdown structure:

# Nathamuni — Source of Truth
## 1. Identity
(name, age, where I live, languages, family context I have shared)
## 2. Professional
(current role and title, employer/industry, years of experience, core expertise, notable achievements)
## 3. The philosophy experiment
(the 6-month self-test of ~50 thinkers' philosophies: which thinkers, what I actually did, what held up, what didn't)
## 4. Calisthenics
(how I started, current skills, training approach, milestones)
## 5. Meditation
(practice, style, how long, what it does for me)
## 6. AI & engineering projects
(every project I have described: name, what it does, tech stack, status, the story behind it)
## 7. My book — "The Silence That Haunts"
(themes, why I wrote it, the writing process, status, what I want readers to feel)
## 8. Tamil identity & roots
(language, culture, places, how it shapes my content)
## 9. Values & worldview
(principles I keep repeating, opinions I hold strongly, what I reject)
## 10. My voice
(5–10 verbatim quotes from my own messages that capture how I actually talk and write)
## 11. Timeline
(dated milestones — education, career, book, training, content)
## 12. Everything else notable
(anything true and interesting that doesn't fit above)

End with a section "## Gaps — ask Nathamuni" listing the most important UNKNOWNs.

Appendix — 5 copy-paste prompts for the remaining text content (ChatGPT / Gemini / Claude)

Each produces content for a specific part of the site. Answer their questions AS YOURSELF; send me the outputs and I wire them in. (The book is excluded — you already gave me the PDF.)

1 · Long-form About page

fills /about
You are my ghostwriter. Interview me with 8 short questions (one at a time) about: my journey into engineering, calisthenics, meditation, why I tested 50 thinkers' philosophies on myself for 6 months, my Tamil roots, and what I want people to get from my content. Then write a 300-word "About" in first person — direct, warm, zero guru-speak, one light Tamil phrase max. My identity line: "☬ Fear lives in one place only... Thats in you Mind🗿 — Distinguished Engr | Author | Calisthenics | Meditation | Memer | AI Architect | Generalist". End with one sentence about my book "The Silence That Haunts".

2 · Projects page case studies

fills /projects
I built AI apps including a local/offline AI mobile app (chat, personas, habit & routine tracking) and "instauser-understanding". Interview me one question at a time: what each project does, the problem it solves, tech stack, hardest challenge, current status, link if public. Then write each up as a 120-word case study: Problem → What I built → Stack → Status. Confident engineer tone, no marketing fluff.

3 · Blog post drafts (repeatable)

fills /blog
Write a 500-word blog post as me based on this idea from one of my reels: [PASTE CAPTION OR IDEA]. Voice: first person, direct, short punchy paragraphs, concrete personal examples over abstract advice, no motivational clichés, ends with one actionable takeaway. Title should be specific, not clickbait. I tested everything I write about on myself first — the writing must reflect that.

4 · SEO bios & platform descriptions

metadata + socials
Create 5 bios from this identity: Nathamuni — Distinguished Engineer, author of "The Silence That Haunts", calisthenics athlete, AI architect, Tamil creator. Site: nathamuni.com (searchable library of my videos on discipline, fitness, AI). Deliver: (1) 155-char SEO meta description, (2) 150-char Instagram bio, (3) YouTube channel description ~500 chars, (4) LinkedIn headline + summary, (5) one-line press bio. Energetic but credible; "tested on myself first" must appear somewhere.

5 · Newsletter welcome (for later)

future email capture
Write a 120-word welcome email as me for "One tested idea, weekly" — a newsletter where each issue takes one idea I verified on myself (discipline systems, calisthenics, AI builds) and gives the practical version. Warm, zero corporate tone, one emoji max, ends by inviting a reply about what they're currently struggling to stay consistent with.
